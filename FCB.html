1.XAVI(Xavier Hernández Creus)


Xavier Hernández Creus, commonly known as Xavi, is a retired professional football player from Spain. He is widely regarded as one of the best midfielders in the history of football and is widely considered as the greatest Spanish player of all time. Xavi was born on January 25, 1980, in Terrassa, Spain. He started playing football at a very young age and joined the youth academy of FC Barcelona, the club he would later represent for most of his career. He made his professional debut for Barcelona in 1998 and quickly established himself as a key player for the team. During his time at Barcelona, Xavi won numerous domestic and international titles. He won eight La Liga titles, four Champions League titles, and two Club World Cup titles, among others. He was also an integral part of the Spanish national team that won the 2010 FIFA World Cup and the 2008 and 2012 UEFA European Championships. Xavi was known for his exceptional passing ability, vision, and control of the midfield. He was also a fantastic leader on and off the pitch and was highly respected by his teammates and opponents alike.In 84 Matches He score 85 Goals and Game winning goals 27. Xavi was particularly renowned for his ability to maintain possession of the ball and dictate the tempo of the game, which was instrumental in Barcelona's success during his time at the club. After leaving Barcelona in 2015, Xavi played for Al Sadd in Qatar before retiring in 2019. Since retiring, Xavi has turned to coaching and is currently the head coach of Qatar Stars League club Al-Sadd. And he also win the FIFA U-20 World Cup in 1999.He's been the FC Barcelona head coach since November 2021.

In 2001, he joined French club Paris Saint-Germain, where he spent two seasons before moving to Spanish giants Barcelona. At Barcelona, Ronaldinho quickly became a fan favourite and helped the team win World Cup in 2002 and two La Liga titles and the UEFA Champions League in 2006. He was also awarded the Ballon d'Or in 2005, given to the best player in Europe and The Best FIFA Men's Player in 2004,2005.Ronaldinho was known for his incredible dribbling skills, technical ability, and creativity on the field. He was also a prolific goal scorer,scoring 110 goals in 250 appearances for Barcelona. In 2008, Ronaldinho joined Italian club AC Milan, where he spent three seasons before returning to Brazil to play for Flamengo and then Atletico Mineiro. He retired from professional football in 2018. Ronaldinho also had a successful international career, representing Brazil at the 2002 and 2006 FIFA World Cups. He was again a key player for Inter Milan in their successful defense of the Serie A title in the 2010-11 season. In 2011, Eto'o moved to Russian club Anzhi Makhachkala, where he became the highest-paid footballer in the world at the time. Eto'o also had a successful international career, representing Cameroon in four FIFA World Cups and winning two African Cup of Nations titles.
